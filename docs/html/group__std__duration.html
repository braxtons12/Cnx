StdDuration represents a positive or negative duration in a particular unit of time (e.g. a (signed) number of seconds, nanoseconds, or years)

Classes

struct StdDuration
StdDuration represents a duration in a particular unit of time A StdDuration occurs in a particular unit of time (e.g. seconds, nanoseconds, or years), and can represent a positive or negative amount of that unit

bool std_duration_equal(StdDuration lhs, StdDuration rhs)

Determines if the two StdDurations are equal.

Parameters
lhs - The lhs StdDuration to compare
rhs - The rhs StdDuration to compare.
Returns whether lhs and rhs are equivalent

Determines if the two StdDurations are equal by comparing them in the most precise period of the two. For example, if lhs's period is std_milliseconds_period and rhs's period is std_seconds_period, rhs will be converted to a StdDuration in std_milliseconds_period before the comparison.
